### **1. Simple Mode**

This is what every new OKX account lands on by default. You can buy and sell spot crypto and hold long options positions, but margin and derivatives are locked. Think of it as the on-ramp: no leverage, no liquidations, no quiz. If you're brand new and just want to buy some BTC or ETH, this is fine. The moment you want to do anything more, you'll want to upgrade.

### **2. Spot and Futures Mode**

The first real step up. You unlock margin, perpetuals, futures, and options alongside spot. The margin engine here is single-currency cross: all trading products settled in the same currency (say, everything USDT-margined) share margin and offset PnL with each other. So your USDT-margined BTC perp and your USDT-margined ETH perp can share collateral, but a USDC-margined position stays separate. Activation requires passing a short trading knowledge quiz — it's not hard, but it forces you to acknowledge you understand leverage risk.

### **3. Multi-Currency Margin Mode**

Here's where the unified account starts earning its name. Multi-currency cross margin lets you share margin and offset PnL across **all** positions, regardless of settlement currency. Your BTC-margined inverse futures can help cover your USDT-margined perp, your ETH spot can serve as collateral for your SOL perp — everything talks to everything. The catch: you need at least $10,000 USD equivalent in unified account equity, and you have to pass the quiz. This is the mode most active cross-market traders gravitate toward.

### **4. Portfolio Margin Mode**

The most advanced mode, built for traders running large hedged positions. Portfolio cross margin does everything multi-currency does, plus it lets derivatives under the **same index** offset margin against each other — meaning a long BTC perp and a short BTC futures on the same underlying can partially cancel out their margin requirements. If you're running complex arbitrage, hedged options books, or basis trades, this is where you want to be. Same $10,000 equity threshold and quiz as multi-currency mode.

- You have no interest in leverage or derivatives

- You want leverage but aren't running complex cross-currency strategies

- You're an active derivatives trader who thinks in terms of total portfolio risk, not per-position risk

**You should use Portfolio Margin if:**
- You run large hedged positions (long perp + short futures on the same underlying, basis trades, options books)
- You want derivatives under the same index to offset margin against each other
- You're operating at a scale where marginal capital efficiency translates into real money
- You have the $10,000 equity threshold and are comfortable with the most sophisticated risk model OKX offers
